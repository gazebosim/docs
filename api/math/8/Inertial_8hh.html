 The Inertial object provides a representation for the mass and inertia matrix of a body B. The components of the inertia matrix are expressed in what we call the "inertial" frame Bi of the body, i.e. the frame in which these inertia components are measured. The inertial frame Bi must be located at the center of mass of the body, but not necessarily aligned with the bodyâ€™s frame.
